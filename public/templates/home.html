You long for a dog.

Okay, so you've been thinking about this for a while now.

A furry friend to help you through those episodes of Walking Dead you love to binge. Or maybe you love the outdoors and would love a hiking companion. Or you need a good watchdog and friend for your children.

Whatever your reason, congratulations on making the stupendous decision to welcome a dog into your life! Now you need to research what kind of dog would work best for you and your family. Would a couch-potato pooch fit your lifestyle best, or a dog that loves to be on the go? Will you have the time to take your dog for a daily walk, or do you need a dog that can meet his exercise needs running around the house? Noise levels, grooming requirements, friendliness...so many things to consider.

Does Breed Matter?

First things first, you need to figure out what breed of dog makes the best sense for you. Dogs will vary within a breed, but you reduce the chances of getting a squirrel-chasing barker when you really wanted a low-key lapdog if you do some research. And here's where ADogforMe.com can help. Click on BREEDS and begin the fun journey of finding the perfect dog for you. You can read all about different breeds, watch videos, and find rescue dogs within your area.

Check out this article on why knowing something about dog breeds can help in your search for the best companion for your lifestyle.

If you have some idea of what criteria your perfect dog friend would meet, then select click BREEDS from the menu and select FILTER LIST to find dog breeds that meet various selection criteria.

And if you would like to save dog breeds to a favorites list, or save and track rescue dogs, then SIGN UP for your own personal account. You can save your research and return back to it at your leisure.

Buy or Rescue?

In a perfect world, this wouldn't even be a question to consider. The reality, however, is that there are many, many dogs out there needing a home. Dogs of every spot, size, and temperment just waiting for a new home!

What? You want a particular breed, and don't want to risk getting an unhealthy or damaged dog? Purchasing from a breeder is no guarantee of health or perfect temperment. There are many reputable breeders out there, but there are irreputable ones as well. Buyer beware and do your research!

And what about that adorable pup in the store window? Well, they need homes also. I would caution you against an impulse buy. Bringing a dog into your life is about long-term commitment and responsibility and shouldn't be an off-the-cuff decision. But if you absolutely fall in love with a bouncy fuzzface, then don't let your friends make you feel guilty about not getting a shelter dog.

I've purchased a dog from a store, bought from breeders, and rescued dogs. They've all had their personality quirks and health concerns, no matter where I found my new companion. The trick is to do your research, talk to the breeder, store or shelter staff, and, if you can, spend time visiting your potential new friend.
